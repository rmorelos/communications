This repository is set up to provide support material for teaching undegraduate students the principles and applications of communication systems. It includes laboratory
experimental procedures, demonstrations with software radios, as well as sets of homework sets and their solutions. The material is arranged as follows:

    


## **I. Fundamentals of RF communications (EE160)**

![image](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/7507e63a-e27b-4928-b76f-d92196331e04)

Table of contents
1. A modern communication system
2. Nonlinear amplifiers, harmonics and two-tone test
3. Harmonics and the continuous-time Fourier series 
4. The continuous-time Fourier transform. Basic properties and transform pairs
5. Frequency shifting and the sampling theorem
6. Amplitude modulation (AM) systems: Suppressed-carrier double-sideband (SC-DSB) AM
7. Synchronous detection of AM signals
8. Envelope detection of AM signals: Large carrier (LC) DSB AM. Modulation index. 
9. Single sideband (SSB) AM
10. Applications of AM: Quadrature modulation and frequency-division multiplexing (FDM)
11. FDM and heterodyne receivers
12. Frequency modulation (FM) systems: Modulation index. 
13. Spectrum of FM signals. Carlson bandwidth.
14. Power spectral density, power content and average power
15. Additive white Gaussian noide (AWGN)
16. Noise performance of AM and FM systems: Signal-to-noise ratio (SNR)
17. Noise figure
18. Pulse sequences and binary modulation schemes usign AM and FM
